WWII Website (From a U.S. Perspective)

Pre U.S. Entry

What caused WWII to break loose?

WWII was caused by the failure to compromise on issues of WWI. The Treaty of Versailles failed in making peace between Nations. Versailles unleashed harsh punishments on Germany such as large war reparations and territorial loss since the blame was put solely on Germany. The harsh punishments significantly led to the rise of Fascism and left Europe vulnerable to future conflicts. The Treaty failed to recognize the root causes of WWI instead of putting all the blame on Germany. Due to the inability to compromise, Fascism and militarism rose.

How did Fascism and the Axis Powers rise?

Due to depression worldwide, desperate measures were taken by some nations. Economic hardships led to the rise of Dictators in Germany, Italy, and Japan in the 1920's and 30's. What also accelerated this rise was nationalist resentments after World War 1. These three countries formed the Axis Powers in 1940 as a treaty alliance.

  • Italy’s Fascist regime seized power in 1922. Benito Mussolini’s Fascist party paved the way for Fascism in Europe. Fascism was ultimately the idea that the people of a nation should glorify it through an aggressive force. The Fascists Marched through Rome looking to impose the will of Mussolini.

  • Germany’s Nazi Party is equivalent to Italy’s Fascist party. The Nazi Party arose due to poor economic conditions along with national resentments to the Treaty of Versailles. Hitler would gain control of the German Legislature in 1933. He led his personal; arm of “Brown’s Shirts.” The Nazi Party mainly attracted disgruntled, unemployed German workers. This was done by using bullying tactics on Jews. Hitler used this intimidation to essentially blame the Jews for the depression. Mussolini’s Fascist party would also become the blueprint for the German Nazi Party.

  • A similar theme of economic hardships lead Japan to go radical as well. Japan’s nationalists and militarists persuaded the emperor to invade China and Southeast Asia for Raw materials (oil, tin, and iron). Political instability also led to this military dictatorship in Japan. Japan’s invasion of Manchuria imposed a big threat to World Peace.

U.S. Before the War

As was most of the world, the United States was in a depression of its own. The Stock Market Crash of 1929 was a start to the Great Depression. This crash was caused by over-speculation along with the unsustainable economic bomb. The Stock Market wouldn’t return to its 1929 high until 1954. Thousands of banks failed across the country. The depression was also caused by overproduction and an agriculture decline. During the depression, 1 in 4 workers were unemployed. President Franklin Delano Roosevelt attempted to fix this with the New Deal. This New Deal would expand the Federal government’s role in the economy. FDR would focus on his 3 R’s: relief of the people, recovery for business and the economy, and reform of American economic institutions. The three R’s would become the three phases of his New Deal programs. The New Deal ultimately put people back to work but at the same time made the government more powerful. It also restores capitalism and gives the people a new sense of hope.

When and why did the U.S. join the war?

The French and British were getting weaker and weaker in the fight. The U.S. was neutral but they had to help the allies stay in the fight. However, the neutrality acts were in Roosevelt’s way. In 1939, FDR persuaded Congress to implement “Cash and Carry” which enabled the British to buy ships in exchange for cash. To get around neutrality a step further, Roosevelt persuaded Congress to have a military ready. The first peacetime military draft would be provided in 1940 through the Selective Training and Service Act. This registered all American men between ages 21 to 25. It also provided training for 1.2 Million troops.

Once France surrendered from the war in 1940, it was just Great Britain to protect the hands of democracy. Matters weren’t getting any better either for the British as they were getting constantly bombed by the Germans. German submarines also threatened to attack in the Atlantic. To get by isolationists, Roosevelt exchanged a deal called Destroyers for Bases which provided 50 U.S. destroyers for the right to build military bases in the Caribbean.

The U.S. was inching closer to the war from a morality standpoint. On January 6th, 1941, Roosevelt addressed Congress with his Four Freedoms speech. He pledged that the U.S. had to defend other countries to defend the “four freedoms.” These “four freedoms” were freedom of speech, freedom of religion, freedom of want, freedom from fear. Public opinion would change even more toward helping Great Britain after Lend Lease. In March 1941, the Lend Lease Act was signed into law to give war supplies to any nation that was vital to U.S. defense. In this case, it was the British. Roosevelt said it would be like lending a neighbor a garden hose to put out the fire. At this point, the cost didn’t matter. Roosevelt also said to the public that they can remove that silly old dollar sign.

With the U.S. soon to enter the war, Roosevelt met with Winston Churchill, Prime Minister of Great Britain. This was a secret meeting where the two leaders drew up the document, the Atlantic Charter. This document would define the principles for peace after the war. The Atlantic Charter also declared that the U.S. and Great Britain fight together as “Christian Soldiers.” In the summer of 1941, the U.S. got in an entanglement overseas with German ships when carrying lend lease materials. Roosevelt ordered the navy to attack German ships on sight. The American destroyer, the Greer, was attacked by a German submarine. With Roosevelts order, the U.S. started an undeclared shooting campaign against the Germans.

Germany isn’t the only reason the U.S. would enter the War. The last straw for the U.S. to declare war was with Japan pulling its strings. When Japan joined the Axis powers in 1940, economic tension occurred between the U.S. and Japan. When the Japanese occupied French Indochina, Roosevelt froze Japanese access to U.S. oil and other materials. Since the oil embargo wasn’t going to end, the Japanese looked elsewhere for oil in a tirade through the Dutch East Indies. The two sides would attempt to reach an agreement on the oil deal but a deal wasn’t reached between Hideki Tojo and the U.S. The U.S. hoped to strengthen the navy in the Pacific before armed confrontation with Japan happened.

The Pacific Fleet for the U.S. anchored in Pearl Harbor, Hawaii. On December 7th, 1941, Japanese planes flew over Pearl Harbor and bombed every ship in sight. Most of the Americans at Pearl Harbor were still asleep. It was approximately 7:55 AM when the attack happened. In less than two hours, 2,400 Americans were killed along with 1,200 wounded. 20 Warships were sunk or damaged. Around 150 airplanes were also destroyed.

Roosevelt described the attack on Pearl Harbor as “a date which will live in infamy.” Roosevelt would also ask Congress to declare war. On December 8th, 1941, Congress declared war on Japan. After the U.S. declared war on Japan, Germany and Italy would honor the Axis Powers by declaring war on the United States. War was on for the U.S.
